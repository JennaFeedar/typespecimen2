Tumba
Tumba text is a serif, script-esque font that was created by Jamie Chang in 2023, who is also the founder of the independent type foundry Manic Type. Based in Toronto, Canada, Manic Type Foundry believes that how we perceive our visual languages directly impacts how we see the world around us. They make typefaces with this in mind, with many of their typefaces possessing personalities and influence all their own; Tumba included.

Tumba draws inspiration from majuscule, large lettering, fonts from the early 4th to 8th centuries. These types of fonts were primarily used by Greek and Latin scribes. It also emulates a fraktur style of text– a type of calligraphic script used primarily to visualize the Latin alphabet. Both of these styles combined create a familiar tone to a body of text while still feeling contemporary and versatile. One might use this font for things like taverns, bed time stories, or scripture.

Tumba text is also a variable script, meaning one can adjust the thickness with a slider on the weight axes. This provides designers with the ability to change their text font into a header font almost instantaneously, making it a valuable asset to any type of project; billboards, signage, or paragraph text– Tumba has you covered.
